## 📖 For Beginners

**What's a "mixing angle"?**  
In the Standard Model, quarks and neutrinos aren't aligned with the forces that act on them. A "mixing angle" measures how much they're rotated relative to each other. The Cabibbo angle (λ ≈ 0.225) is the most famous — it tells you how often a strange quark decays via the weak force compared to a down quark.

**What does "zero free parameters" mean?**  
Most physics theories have knobs you turn to match experiments — like a radio you tune until the sound is clear. This theory claims the knobs are already locked to specific values by the mathematical structure itself. You don't tune anything. You just compute.

**What happens if JUNO measures Normal Ordering?**  
The IO prediction is falsified. The framework's seesaw layer would be wrong. The core algebraic results (gauge group, 3 generations, θ_QCD=0) would survive, but the theory as a complete zero-parameter package would need major revision or abandonment. That's how science works — theories that make hard predictions can be killed by experiment. Theories that don't, can't.

**What happens if JUNO measures Inverted Ordering?**  
Then this paper becomes the first zero-parameter theory in history to have its most counter-consensus prediction confirmed by experiment. The Standard Model's 19 free parameters would have a common algebraic origin.
