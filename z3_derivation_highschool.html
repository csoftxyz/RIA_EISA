Three Concepts — One Minute Each

Don't skip — every later step depends on these

🧭 A vector = an arrow with direction and length
Stand in the middle of a room. Point at the ceiling = [0,0,1] (up).
Point forward = [1,0,0] (ahead). Point right = [0,1,0] (sideways).
Three numbers [x,y,z] = how far you go in each direction.
🔄 Z₃ symmetry = rotate three times to come full circle
Picture an equilateral triangle. Rotate 120° (one-third turn) — looks identical.
Rotate again — still identical. Three rotations = 360° = back to start.
This "cycle of three" symmetry is called Z₃.
📐 Dot product = how "aligned" two arrows are
Same direction → dot product positive. Perpendicular → zero. Opposite → negative.
L² = arrow length squared = "how far this vector is from the origin."
